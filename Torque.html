Aircraft Schedule User Guide



What is Aircraft Schedule?

Aircraft Schedule is a Torque Suite application that provides Planning and Scheduling maintainers with a tool to plan and build maintenance and operations schedules that can be approved and distributed within an organization. As part of the Torque Suite applications, Aircraft Schedule shares data with other Torque applications to reduce the need for redundant data entry.


Who should use Aircraft Schedule?

  • Planning and Scheduling (P&S) Maintainers

  • AMU/Unit Leadership


    How can I use Aircraft Schedule?

  • Directly import maintenance and flying schedule reports

    • We currently import the following reports: mmPairs, PRA, G081, GTIMS, some PEX, TMS

  • Schedule Maintenance or Operations events by using our optimization tool or manually plotting events

  • Display schedules in weekly and monthly checkerboard

  • Display scheduled events statistics in user-friendly tabs

  • Create an easily editable all-in-one schedule that allows direct editing vs. copying/pasting from multiple spreadsheets

  • Share printable schedules using our "Landscape" and "Portrait" print options

  • Export schedules to an Excel document to share digitally

  • Present schedules in maintenance or operations meetings using our "Presentation View"

      1. Maintenance Events


        Our application currently imports mmPAIRs, PRA, and G081 reports exported to Excel. Imported events will appear under the MX Side Panel once imported successfully.


        To manually create a Maintenance Event:


        • Select the + icon from the upper right hand corner of the screen.

        • Select MX Event or G081 Event.

          • G081 specific events are as follows:

            • CON - Contingency

            • EO - Event Occurence

            • INSP - Inspection

        • Select your event from the drop-down menu.

          • If you don't see your event listed, select other.

        • Fill out information as needed.

        • At the bottom of the side panel you have the option to Flag, Ground, or Lock your event.

          • For more explanation on locked events, skip to Locked Events.

        • Click Save and you should see your event on the checkerboard.

          • If the event does not have a tail assigned, the event will be in the MX side panel.

            You can toggle ops events as a Placeholder event which will allow you to keep the sortie's line number assignment in the checkerboard. However, this will prevent line number assignment within the print view.


            Multi-day Ops


            Multi-day ops is a feature that now allows users to schedule sorties that are multiple days or weeks long. This feature is available for all ops events except for spares.


            Known Issues:


        • Takeoffs & Landings on stats tab, and presentation calculations are displaying on the day after the takeoff date.


          How Do Multi-day Ops Effect My Schedule?


        • Turn patterns: multi-day ops with a line number will appear in the turn pattern of the takeoff day. Not the landing day.

        • Stats Tab: multi-day ops with or without a line number will appear in your stats but only in the flyers and total stats. Again this is only for the takeoff day.

        • Ops Tab: multi-day ops with a line number will appear in the ops tab on the all the days it is flying through. For example: line 100 is taking off Monday and returns Wednesday. Line 100 will appear on the Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday drop downs.

        • Print: multi-day ops will appear on the checkerboard, with or without a line number.

          • Stats print out: all multi-day ops will appear in the "flyers" and "total" stat calculations. But only for the takeoff day.

          • Fly pages: only on the day the sortie is taking off, with or without a line number. Flying events without a line number will appear in the "Go -1" section.

        • Presentation view: multi-day ops will appear on the checkerboard, with or without a line number.

          • Stats presentation view: all multi-day ops will appear in the "flyers" and "total" stat calculations. But only for the takeoff day.

          • Takeoffs and landings presentation view: please note the known issue listed above. The multi-day ops events with a take off and land time field will be displayed on the take off day.

    5. Drag And Drop

      Drag and drop is a feature that allows users to quickly manipulate their schedule by clicking and dragging mx or ops events across the checkerboard to a new spot. Users can also drag mx or ops events from the side panel onto the checkerboard.


      Interactions:


      1. Drag a mx or ops event across the checkerboard to a new spot.

      2. Drag a mx or ops event from the side panel onto the checkerboard.

        • This can be done from all drop down selections of the mx or ops side panel.

        • Note: dragging events that already exist on the checkerboard from the side panel will not duplicate the event. It will just re-plot the event to the new spot.

      3. Ops events can move freely to a new tail or day on the checkerboard.

      4. Maintenance events are constrained to move within their tail.

      5. You can move a mx or ops event to another spot on the checkerboard that is already populated with events.

      6. Drag an entire grouped event e.g., F2, SI4, etc. as a whole to a new spot on the checkerboard.

      7. Drag single events out of grouped events. This can be done by clicking on the grouped event and letting the popover card appear. Then from the popover card you can grab a single event and move it out of the group to a new spot on the checkerboard.

      What drag and drop can't perform:


      • Drag mx or ops event back to the side panel to unplot the event.

      • Drag mx or ops events into the past.

      • Drag a mx event to a different tail than the one it's assigned to.


        Locked events:


      • As a reminder, locked events are only for optimization. When an event is locked it means the optimization feature shouldn't move it from it's current spot. Users do have the ability to edit or move locked events as they please.

      • You can drag locked mx or ops events to a new spot on the checkerboard.

      • You can drag mx or ops events onto or off locked All Day events.


        Why isn't an event moving to a new spot?


      • This may be because you are trying to move the event into the past. Please double check the date you are trying to drag the event to.

      • If it's a maintenance event, double check that you are dragging it to is assigned tail.


    6. Locked Events

      We now have a locking feature for Maintenance, Operations, and All Day events. This prevents our optimization tool from moving these locked down events and instead create a schedule around them.


      • How to lock MX and OPS events:

        • Select an event on the checkerboard.

        • Select the edit icon on the info popout card.

        • The OPS or MX side panel will then pop out for the event you selected.

        • Scroll to the bottom of the side panel.

        • Select the "Locked" toggle so the slider moves to the right side.

        • Scroll back to the top of the side panel and hit the "SAVE" button.

      • All Day Events & Holidays:

        • All day events and Holidays are locked by default to prevent optimization from scheduling on those days.

          • The other option for All Day events is NOT locked by default.

        • To unlock All Day Events:

          • Select the all day event you'd like to lock.

          • Go over to the all day event side panel.

          • Select the "Locked" toggle so it slides to the right.

          • Select the "SAVE" button.

          • The existing all day event should now have a "lock" icon to the left of the title.

        • To Unlock a Holiday Event:

          • You can unlock holidays by clicking on "Holiday" right below the date and toggling "Locked" to off.


      NOTE: UNDER VERY RARE CIRCUMSTANCES WILL LOCKED EVENTS MOVE DUE TO THE OPTIMIZATION'S CALCULATIONS. WE ARE AWARE OF THIS AND CURRENTLY RESEARCHING A FUTURE FIX.


  1. Optimization

    Optimization is a tool that helps create a schedule quickly while also following many of the rules schedules must adhere to. What's happening in the background is math combined with linear programming in order to create an optimal schedule. This optimization model is based on data gathered by conducting research with fellow airmen.


    The chart below shows what the optimization is prioritizing when scheduling events:


    • The criteria block highlighted in green takes the highest priority.

    • The criteria block highlighted in blue takes medium priority.

    • The criteria block highlighted in red takes the lowest priority.


    The optimization is undergoing iterative research to ensure criteria is prioritized appropriately.


    Light Re-Optimization (LRO) Vs Optimization

    Optimization: In AV Schedule, selecting [Full optimization-option name here] will allow the optimizer to reschedule all unlocked events in order to generate the best schedule. This means that all events on the checkerboard that are not locked could move if the software believes it can improve the schedule in terms of fitting all requested events, improving the timing of maintenance, and/or balancing flight-hours across the squadron.


    Light re-optimization: In AV Schedule, selecting "Light re-optimization" means the optimizer will respect all locked events and do its best to keep the remaining previously scheduled unlocked events in their existing checker-board placement. Any requested events that are not already on the schedule or locked will be added to the existing schedule around the locked and pre-scheduled events as best possible. In some cases, the optimizer may move unlocked events that were on the schedule - but only if it is necessary to fit all new events or changes. Example uses of "Light re­ optimization" would be to quickly adapt the schedule mid-week after an important maintenance resource goes down or new sorties are added.

    5. How to Export a PRA report from IMDS

  1. Open IMDS.


  2. Navigate to IMDS screen 396.


  3. From the "Select Type Of File" dropdown select Excel(csv).


  4. In the "Date Options" area input your desired start date for the maintenance events you want to import into Aircraft Schedule.


  5. Then input the number of days you want maintenance for in the "Number Of Days" input box.

  6. Once that is done make sure you are exporting for the correct MOS.


  7. Hit the "Xmit" button to generate the report.


  8. Open up the file in Excel and re-save the as a ".xis" or ".xlsx" file format.


*Please note that when you are viewing the PRA file in excel please double check two things for correct formatting or else the file will not import correctly:


  1. Tail numbers in the "Key" column match your tail numbers listed in Aircraft Schedule exactly. Our app parser matches the "Key" to the tail number, not the AF Number or Aircraft ID data fields. Ex: your one of your tails has the following format: "1023" but your PRA "Key" column shows: " F1006D A A1023" this will not import.


  2. That the Due Dates are in the following format DD-MMM-YY. Month needs to be abbreviated and not the number.
